Dance Shoes 101

Here you can find out which shoes will work best depending on which genre you are doing!

Pointe Shoes

Pointe shoes are essentially a ballet slipper that is fitted with a thick, reinforced toe usually made out of wood to enable the ballet dancer to "dance on their toes". You can use these ONLY for Pointe routines.

Tap Shoes

Tap shoes have attached metal plates at the toe and heel to make a "tapping". These should be used ONLY for Tap routines.

Character Shoes

Character shoes are leather shoes with anywhere from one to three inch heels. These should be used ONLY for Musical Theatre routines.

Jazz Shoes

Jazz shoes are available in a variety of styles with varying features. They can slip on, tie, be black, or even tan colored. These could be worn for EITHER Jazz or Pom routines.

Dance Footies

Dance Footies are pretty much "half shoes". They have a synthetic leather foot pad that allows for easy turning. There are also elastic grips to the foot to prevent slipping.These should be worn in ONLY Lyrical or Modern routines.

Ballet Slippers

Ballet Slippers are simmilar to Dance Footies; however, they are an entire shoe. They are made of genuine leather and cotton lining. These should be used for EITHER Pre Pointe or Ballet routines.

Dance Sneakers

Dance Sneakers are like regular black tennis shoes; however, they have a more flexible arch. These should be used for ONLY Hip Hop routines.
